All about Jellyfish

Jellyfish are aquatic creatures that have existed for millions of years in the depth of the ocean. Though the term "fish" appears in their name, they are not fish, but rather a type of zooplankton. Jellyfish are organisms made up of 95% water and have a bell-shaped structure with tentacles. There are around 1000 different species of jellyfish that come in all shapes and sizes. Some jellyfish can be the size of whales, whereas others can be as small as a grain of rice. Jellyfish have no bones but have a neural net that forms a nervous system. The nervous system allows them to detect other sea creatures and helps them sting these animals to prey or defend themselves. The sting is venomous and can kill a human in under 5 minutes. Jellyfish eat, absorb nutrients and produce water through their bell-shaped top. They are carnivores and often prey and small fish. Unique features of jellyfish include…

  • 360° vision
  • Changing color depending moods
  • Short lives (2↔3 years)
  • Long lives (immortal)
  • Laying 40000+ eggs at a time
  • Surviving in any environment underwater (not many predators, increasing population)
